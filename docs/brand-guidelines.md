# Brand Guidelines - Sourdough Bakery

## 1) Purpose and Scope
This document defines the visual and emotional identity of the Sourdough Bakery web application.
It is the creative reference for all public pages, shop pages, customer account screens, and admin-facing UI.

### Project Context
- Product type: Artisan bakery website with integrated ecommerce and admin management
- Brand goal: Present handcrafted sourdough and baked goods in a way that feels warm, trustworthy, educational, and premium
- UX goal: Encourage calm exploration, appetite, trust, and eventual conversion without aggressive selling
- Design goal: Build a cohesive bakery identity that feels authentic rather than decorative or generic

## 2) Brand Mission
Sourdough Bakery is a modern artisan bakery dedicated to naturally fermented bread and handcrafted baked goods.

The brand combines traditional baking techniques with a clean contemporary presentation. The result should feel honest, calm, and made with care.

The mission is not simply to sell bread, but to inspire people to rediscover:
- slow fermentation
- natural ingredients
- handmade quality
- the comfort of freshly baked food

## 3) Brand Personality
The brand should consistently feel:
- Warm
- Authentic
- Honest
- Welcoming
- Calm
- Family-oriented
- Crafted with care
- Natural
- Timeless
- Premium without feeling exclusive

## 4) Emotional Experience
Every page should communicate warmth and craftsmanship.

Visitors should immediately feel:
- the aroma of freshly baked bread
- comfort and trust
- natural ingredients and slow fermentation
- handmade quality
- quality over quantity
- a sense of calm browsing rather than rushed shopping

The site should invite people to stay, discover, and learn.
It should feel like visiting a real artisan bakery, not a fast retail catalog.

## 5) Core Visual Identity
The overall visual language should be:
- Clean
- Elegant
- Minimalist
- Editorial
- Artisan bakery inspired
- Spacious
- Photography-led

Guiding principle:
- Prefer quiet confidence over loud design

Avoid:
- cluttered sections
- excessive decoration
- high-saturation accents
- generic ecommerce styling
- overly glossy or tech-like interfaces

## 6) Color Direction
### 6.1 Primary Palette
Primary surfaces should stay rooted in warm neutrals:
- Warm white
- Cream
- Linen
- Soft beige

### 6.2 Accent Palette
Accents should support warmth and craftsmanship:
- Light brown
- Dark brown
- Muted olive (small accents only)
- Terracotta (small accents only)

### 6.3 Color Usage Rules
- Neutral surfaces should dominate the interface
- Brown tones should guide emphasis, structure, and CTA hierarchy
- Olive and terracotta should be used sparingly for seasonal warmth, not as dominant brand colors
- Avoid saturated primaries, neon colors, or playful candy-like palettes

## 7) Typography
Typography should feel elegant, readable, and confident.

### 7.1 Headings
Headings should feel:
- handcrafted
- premium
- calm
- editorial

### 7.2 Body Text
Body text should remain:
- simple
- readable
- lightly educational
- visually quiet

### 7.3 Typography Rules
- Prefer large headings with strong breathing room
- Maintain comfortable line height
- Use limited font variation across the app
- Keep emphasis subtle; avoid too much bold text in a single view
- Marketing pages can feel more editorial, but product and account pages must remain easy to scan

## 8) Photography Style
Photography should always look natural and believable.

Preferred imagery includes:
- artisan sourdough bread
- flaky croissants
- rustic baguettes
- pastries and seasonal baked goods
- flour, linen, wooden boards, fresh ingredients
- natural daylight
- hands shaping dough
- bakery work surfaces and ovens

Avoid:
- harsh studio lighting
- artificial food styling
- generic stock photos that feel staged
- unrealistic color grading
- overly busy image collages

Images should communicate authenticity first, beauty second.

## 9) Layout Principles
The interface should breathe.

Use:
- generous vertical spacing
- clear section rhythm
- balanced composition
- large margins and padding
- deliberate alignment
- a small number of strong elements per section

Avoid:
- cramped cards
- dense text walls on landing sections
- too many competing visual focal points
- stacking several CTAs in one block

### Layout Rule
When in doubt, remove one element instead of adding one more.

## 10) UI Component Principles
Components should feel soft, welcoming, and refined.

### Preferred UI language
- Rounded corners
- Soft shadows
- Light borders
- Minimal motion
- Clean Bootstrap-based layout
- Strong responsive behavior
- Accessible focus states

### Avoid
- sharp, aggressive card shapes everywhere
- overly heavy shadows
- oversized badges or excessive icon decoration
- highly animated components

## 11) Buttons and CTAs
Buttons should invite exploration, not pressure users.

Preferred CTA tone:
- Discover Our Bread
- Explore the Bakery
- Learn More
- Read the Story
- View Products

Avoid:
- Buy Now!!!
- Hurry / urgency tactics
- sales-heavy or aggressive phrasing

### CTA Rules
- One clear primary CTA per block when possible
- Secondary CTAs should feel supportive, not competitive
- Keep button labels calm, descriptive, and human

## 12) Cards
Cards should prioritize photography and clarity.

Each content or product card should generally include:
- generous image area
- short meaningful text
- one clear next step
- subtle hover response

Card styling should remain visually light.
Do not overload cards with too many labels, icons, buttons, or metadata.

## 13) Motion and Interaction
Motion should be subtle and purposeful.

Use:
- gentle hover lifts
- soft fades or reveals
- small directional motion for affordances

Avoid:
- constant movement
- dramatic parallax
- large bounce effects
- animation that competes with content

Motion should support calm exploration, not excitement.

## 14) Iconography
Icons should feel understated and functional.

Prefer:
- thin or medium-weight strokes
- simple shapes
- supporting role only

Avoid:
- cartoonish icons
- highly decorative icon packs
- icons that become the focal point over photography or typography

## 15) Content Rhythm and Copy Tone
Write like a passionate artisan baker who understands their craft.

The voice should feel:
- warm
- educational
- calm
- friendly
- confident
- grounded

Avoid:
- marketing cliches
- exaggerated claims
- overly polished corporate language
- artificial luxury tone

### Copy Structure Rules
- Headlines should be short and evocative
- Supporting text should be calm and informative
- Product text should be appetizing and clear
- Long pages should be broken into readable sections with breathing room

## 16) Experience Differences by Page Type
### 16.1 Storytelling Pages
Examples:
- Home
- About Us
- About Sourdough
- Good to Know
- Recipes

These pages may be more editorial, image-led, and atmospheric.

### 16.2 Ecommerce Pages
Examples:
- Products
- Product Details
- Cart
- Checkout

These pages should still feel warm and premium, but must be:
- faster to scan
- more structured
- more conversion-oriented
- less decorative than storytelling pages

### 16.3 Admin Pages
Admin UI should remain visually consistent with the brand, but more neutral and functional.

Admin screens should prioritize:
- clarity
- hierarchy
- scannability
- form and table usability

Do not over-style admin interfaces at the cost of usability.

## 17) Accessibility Standards
Accessibility is part of product quality, not an optional enhancement.

Always maintain:
- readable contrast
- semantic HTML
- descriptive alt text
- keyboard accessibility
- clear focus states
- responsive layouts
- readable font sizes and spacing

If a design choice reduces clarity or accessibility, reject it.

## 18) Implementation Rules for This Project
While implementing sections and components:
- Reuse existing tokens and layout utilities first
- Keep components modular and focused
- Use Bootstrap grid and responsive utilities where helpful
- Prefer image-first composition for customer-facing sections
- Keep section spacing consistent across pages
- Design for desktop, tablet, and mobile from the start
- Avoid introducing styles that conflict with the established warm-neutral system

## 19) Decision Framework
Whenever a design decision is unclear, prefer:
- simplicity over complexity
- quality over quantity
- authenticity over decoration
- craftsmanship over mass production
- spaciousness over density
- clarity over novelty

## 20) Non-Negotiable Summary
These rules should not drift across the project:
- Warm neutral palette first
- Photography before ornament
- Spacious layouts over crowded layouts
- Elegant headings, simple body text
- Soft shadows, soft corners, minimal motion
- Calm, educational, human voice
- Accessibility is mandatory
- The site should feel like a real artisan bakery, not a generic online shop

## 21) Quick Implementation Checklist
Use this checklist when building any new section:
- Does it feel warm, calm, and handcrafted?
- Is the layout spacious enough?
- Is photography doing most of the visual work?
- Are headings elegant and readable?
- Is the CTA clear but not aggressive?
- Is the section accessible by keyboard and readable on mobile?
- Does it match the existing design tokens and UI language?
- Would this still feel authentic if shown without decorative extras?
