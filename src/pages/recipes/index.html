Beginner Guide

How to Make Your Own Sourdough Starter

A sourdough starter is a living culture of wild yeast and bacteria that gives sourdough bread its distinctive flavour and lift. With just flour, water, and patience, you can grow your own from scratch in 5–7 days.

Time: 5–7 days (10 min/day) Yield: 1 active starter (~200 g) Difficulty: Beginner

What You Need

  • Whole wheat or rye flour (for Day 1–2)
  • All-purpose or bread flour (for ongoing feedings)
  • Unchlorinated water (filtered or left overnight)
  • A clean glass jar (500 ml or larger)
  • A loose lid, cloth, or cling film with holes
  • A kitchen scale

Step-by-Step Method

  1. Day 1 — Mix: Combine 50 g whole wheat flour and 50 g water (room temperature) in a clean jar. Stir well until no dry flour remains. Cover loosely and leave at room temperature (21–24 °C).
  2. Days 2–3 — Discard and feed: Discard half the starter (~50 g). Add 50 g all-purpose flour and 50 g water. Stir well, cover, and leave 24 h. You may see small bubbles beginning to form.
  3. Days 4–5 — Activity rises: Repeat the discard and feed process. The starter should now smell slightly tangy. Bubbles and a slight dome are signs of healthy fermentation.
  4. Days 6–7 — Ready test: Your starter is active when it doubles in size within 4–8 hours of a feeding and passes the float test — drop a small spoonful into water; if it floats, it is ready to bake with.
  5. Ongoing maintenance: Feed once a day at room temperature, or weekly in the fridge if you bake less often.

Baker's Tips

  • Warmer environments speed up fermentation; cooler rooms slow it down.
  • A consistent feed time each day helps build a reliable starter rhythm.
  • Use a rubber band to mark rise level so you can measure growth easily.
  • If pink or orange streaks appear, discard and start fresh — that is mould.

Easy Recipe

Garlic Sourdough Crackers

A brilliant way to use sourdough discard — these thin, crispy crackers are loaded with roasted garlic flavour and a satisfying crunch. Ready in under an hour and impossible to stop eating.

Prep: 10 min Bake: 25–30 min at 180 °C Yield: ~40 crackers Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 200 g sourdough discard (unfed starter)
  • 60 g all-purpose flour
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely grated or minced
  • 1 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary or mixed herbs (optional)
  • Flaky salt and extra herbs for topping

Method

  1. Mix the dough: In a bowl, combine sourdough discard, flour, olive oil, garlic, salt, and herbs. Stir until a smooth, slightly sticky dough forms.
  2. Roll thin: Place the dough between two sheets of baking parchment. Roll out as thin as possible — aim for 2–3 mm. Thin crackers will be crispier and more even.
  3. Score and top: Remove the top sheet of parchment. Cut the dough into rough rectangles or squares with a pizza cutter or sharp knife. Brush lightly with olive oil and scatter flaky salt and extra herbs over the top.
  4. Bake: Transfer the crackers (still on parchment) to a baking sheet. Bake at 180 °C (160 °C fan / gas 4) for 25–30 minutes until golden and crisp. Edges will colour first — watch carefully in the last 5 minutes.
  5. Cool and store: Leave to cool on the tray. They will crisp up further as they cool. Store in an airtight tin for up to a week.

Baker's Tips

  • Use discard straight from the fridge — no need to bring to room temperature.
  • Add seeds (sesame, poppy, caraway) for extra flavour and crunch.
  • For a richer flavour, substitute 15 g of the olive oil with garlic-infused oil.
  • If your discard is very liquid, reduce olive oil to 20 g so the dough is rollable.
